### I. Framing-bias correction #4 — modal-overreach lesson

**Source event:** Claude follow-up writeup in §D conversation translated Stephen's verbatim "the collapse of spacetime vacuum *may* trigger disassociation of baryonic matter and reverse baryogenisis *by some means*" into "Candidate mechanism: collapse of spacetime vacuum triggers disassociation of baryonic matter and reverse baryogenesis by some means; other collapse mechanisms permitted; specific mechanism intentionally underspecified at postulate level". Lost the "may". Promoted vacuum collapse from possibility/outcome to mechanism level. Detected by Stephen "STOP!" correction.

**Resolution:** Postulate 2 refined with correct hierarchy and modal status. Introduction Letter v2 issued with correct wording. Methodology lesson recorded.

**Methodology lesson — modal overreach as distinct failure mode:**

When translating engineering language to scientific language for the corpus, modal status (may, possibly, by some means) must be preserved at the same precision used in the source. The specification-level chain (postulate → endpoint event → outcome → consequence) must be preserved at the level committed, not promoted to higher commitment levels in the translation.

This is a distinct failure mode from prior framing-bias corrections:
- Correction #1 (Amendment 5): Rusakov v1 pro-UMF framing → v2 neutral methodology. Failure mode: pre-judging external paper's interpretive stance by framing assumption.
- Correction #2 (Amendment 6): Topic 4 anti-prior-thread overreach → Topic 5a held appropriately. Failure mode: pre-judging prior-thread content's correctness by anti-framing without body verification.
- Correction #3 (Amendment 7): Research Plan v0.3 Item 1 "extended beyond α_s to variant fundamental constants more broadly" → v0.4 tightening. Failure mode: pre-judging Stephen's *content scope* by framing extension beyond what Stephen actually said.
- **Correction #4 (this Amendment 8): Claude follow-up writeup "Candidate mechanism: collapse of spacetime vacuum triggers..." → Stephen STOP correction → Postulate 2 refined with correct modal status. Failure mode: promoting Stephen's modal status (may, possibly, by some means) and specification-level (possibility/consequence/outcome) to higher commitment levels in translation.**

The four corrections share the structure of *over-specification of Stephen's position* but differ in axis:
- Correction #1: over-specification of *interpretive stance* (Rusakov framed as anti-UMF before verification)
- Correction #2: over-specification of *prior-thread content correctness* (Claude judged prior thread wrong before verification)
- Correction #3: over-specification of *content scope* (Stephen position extended beyond stated)
- Correction #4: over-specification of *modal status and specification level* (possibilities promoted to mechanisms; "may" lost in translation)

### J. Controlled-synthesis-injection vs uncontrolled-synthesis-creep terminology

**Stephen original framing (Introduction Letter v1):** "(this is intentional controlled synthesis injection)" and "We must actively prevent inadvertent 'synthesis creep', ie, uncontrolled synthesis injection."

**Standing-rule formalisation in Amendment 8:** The distinction is now operative standing methodology:
- **Controlled synthesis injection:** Stephen-explicit, transparent, recorded addition of new UMF postulates or commitments. Documented in introduction letter and/or VC amendment. Audit-trail captures the addition.
- **Uncontrolled synthesis creep:** Inadvertent introduction of UMF framings, commitments, or assumptions into contexts where they should not propagate. Failure mode to be actively guarded against. Particularly applies to LLM contexts where UMF-framed corpus may inadvertently shape interpretation in non-UMF or framework-neutral work.
