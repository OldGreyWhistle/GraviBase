## (a) UMF compatibility — CENTRAL FINDINGS

### A.1 Gravitational permittivity ε(ρ): direct conceptual analogue to UMF assumption 4

Refracted Gravity introduces **gravitational permittivity ε(ρ)** — a monotonic function of local mass density — directly inspired by electromagnetic permittivity in matter (Maxwell electrodynamics tradition).

**Modified Poisson equation:** ∇·[ε(ρ)∇Φ] = 4πGρ

Reduces to standard Poisson when ε(ρ) = 1 (high-density limit). ε(ρ) departs from unity in low-density environments (galactic outskirts; intergalactic medium; voids), producing field-line refraction.

**UMF compatibility:** Direct conceptual analogue to UMF v0.7 §3.1 assumption 4 framework where ε₀/μ₀ are admitted to vary under variant geometry (Stephen Q3 adjudication 25 May 2026). RG provides:
- Mainstream-physics precedent for gravity-coupled permittivity treatment (RG's gravitational ε(ρ); UMF's geometry-coupled ε₀/μ₀)
- Active 2016-2026 research programme with empirical engagement (galactic + cluster scales)
- Covariant scalar-tensor extension (CRG, Sanna et al. 2023) providing relativistic embedding

### A.2 Field-line refraction = gravity-field geometric displacement (Stephen Issue 2 highest fit)

**STRONGEST DIRECT MATCH for UMF gravity-mass spatial-offset structure identified across entire trial corpus.**

RG mechanism: field lines literally refract at density-gradient boundaries. In low-density regions (galactic outskirts), field lines bend away from the radial direction, producing a gravitational field that effectively appears displaced from the mass source. Standard Newtonian field lines point radially; RG field lines refract toward higher-density regions.

**Result:** Gravity-field structure geometrically displaced from baryonic mass distribution — exactly the UMF gravity-mass spatial-offset structure Stephen flagged as "missing from the model with no related research that I can find" (Issue 2, 25 May 2026).

**Important mechanistic distinction:**
- RG mechanism: classical field-line refraction at density-gradient boundaries (mass-density-driven)
- UMF mechanism: phase-conjugate-wavefront-mediated geometric offset (wavefront-driven)
- Different mechanism, structurally analogous outcome
- UMF technical development (Item 6 §11.3 in scaffold v2) must specify the phase-conjugate-wavefront mechanism distinctly from RG's mass-density-gradient mechanism

### A.3 Empirical-engagement substantive

RG has substantive empirical engagement:
- **DiskMass Survey 30 disk galaxies** (Cesare et al. 2020 *A&A* 637:A70) — RG reproduces rotation curves with mass-to-light ratios consistent with stellar population synthesis models and disk-scale heights consistent with edge-on observations

**Honest disclosure:** No 5σ confirmation of RG; mixed empirical results (some galaxies fit well, some show residual scatter inconsistent with observations per Cesare 2024 review verbatim: "with residuals correlating with some galaxies properties and with a too large intrinsic scatter, in contrast with observations, which requires further investigation"). Mainstream-physics engagement substantive; consensus not yet reached.
