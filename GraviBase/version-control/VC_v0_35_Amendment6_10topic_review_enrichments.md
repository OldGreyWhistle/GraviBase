## Section D — Methodological reflection: framing-bias bidirectionality lesson

**Augmenting Amendment 5 §D framing-bias lesson:**

Amendment 5 §D recorded the Rusakov v1→v2 framing-bias correction as a "pro-UMF framing bias" lesson, with five specific artefact types identified (asymmetric labelling, artificial count parity, alignment-hypothesis framing, soft-hedging of decisive arguments, options-with-recommendation structure). The lesson was framed as a methodology requirement to assess papers on their own terms first.

**Topic 4 verification process exposed the symmetric inverse failure mode:**

During the 10-topic resumption pass, Topic 4 (arXiv:2402.16136 Marino et al. 2024 Perspective) returned an abstract that did not contain the trial-attributed verbatim. Claude escalated this finding to "**Issue 1 — Verified provenance error**" in the user-facing message before having body access (rate-limit-blocked). The honest framing should have been "**verbatim NOT in abstract; body verification needed**" — absence-from-abstract is not absence-from-paper.

Stephen supplied body extract within one message turn; verbatim was confirmed present in the paper body §IIB. Attribution was correct all along.

**Diagnosis:**

Where Rusakov v1 exhibited *pro-UMF* framing bias (overweighting evidence favourable to UMF), the Topic 4 escalation exhibited *anti-prior-thread* framing bias (overweighting evidence suggesting prior-thread error). Both biases share a common structure: pre-judging the question by the framing chosen before evidence is complete. The Rusakov v1 bias pre-judged via "does this paper support UMF?" framing; the Topic 4 escalation pre-judged via "is the prior thread wrong?" framing.

**Topic 5a process self-correction:**

Following the Topic 4 lesson, Topic 5a (Cosmic Rush Hour arXiv:2509.19427) presented the same pattern: trial-attributed verbatim not in abstract; body check needed. This time the framing was held appropriately at "PARTIAL — body check needed" rather than escalated. Stephen supplied body extract; verbatim was confirmed present in literature-review section. Attribution was correct.

**Methodology lesson (augmented for trial integrity):**

Honest-disclosure stance applies to **framing structure** in **both directions**:
- Pro-UMF framing pre-judges by alignment-hypothesis structure
- Anti-prior-thread framing pre-judges by error-finding structure
- The neutral approach holds verification-pending findings at their actual verification status without escalating to either direction

**Application going forward:** All future paper reviews and provenance checks in trial should distinguish:
- **Verified absence** (paper accessed in full; specific content confirmed absent)
- **Verification pending** (paper not fully accessed; absence-from-abstract or absence-from-summary noted but body not verified)
- **Verified presence** (paper accessed in full; specific content confirmed present)

Promoting "verification pending" to "verified absence" requires actual body access. This is now a standing methodology requirement alongside the Amendment 5 lesson and prior standing rules.

**Application to current Amendment:** All five 10-topic verifications in this Amendment distinguish verification status explicitly per the table in §A.1. Topic 4 and Topic 5a are recorded as "VERIFIED" only after Stephen-supplied body extracts confirmed verbatim presence. No verifications were promoted to "VERIFIED" on abstract evidence alone where a verbatim body check was at issue.
