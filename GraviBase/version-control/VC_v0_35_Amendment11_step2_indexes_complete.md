**Original characterisation (Amendment 10 §3.E; Scaffold v8 §5.2 [v7 honest disclosure]):** "Three distinct n_B/s or η values appear across the 2026 cluster... Samaddar-Singh and Liu-Qin-Bian 9.42 × 10⁻¹¹ / 8.8 × 10⁻¹¹."

**Sharpening:** Samaddar-Singh adopts η_B/s ≃ 9.42 × 10⁻¹¹ (via Planck 2016 [55] + Kolb-Turner 1990 [56] textbook). Cross-cluster positioning of this value:

- **Within** Davoudiasl 2004 corpus range: 9.2 ± 0.6 × 10⁻¹¹ → upper bound 9.8; Samaddar-Singh 9.42 within range
- **Above** Pereira 2026 upper bound: 8.8 + 0.6 = 9.4; Samaddar-Singh 9.42 just outside
- **At upper edge** of Whittingham 2026 BBN range: 8.2–9.4; Samaddar-Singh 9.42 at upper edge
- **Above** Whittingham 2026 CMB value: 8.65 + 0.09 = 8.74; Samaddar-Singh significantly outside CMB-precision range
- **Highest** observational-target value in 2026 cluster

The choice is defensible against the older Planck 2016 + Kolb-Turner 1990 textbook target Samaddar-Singh cites. However, it is the highest target in the contemporaneous cluster and contributes to the observational-target spread independently of the parameter-tuning concerns. Sharpening recorded in Index 3 MU-001 Values-across-corpus table and Comparability notes.

This sharpening is part of honest-disclosure correction #3 (n_B/s observational-target evolution across 2026 cluster); not a new correction, but a refinement of the v7 disclosure for v8-integrated content.
