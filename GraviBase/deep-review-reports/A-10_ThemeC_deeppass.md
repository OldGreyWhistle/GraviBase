## (d) Divergences (5)

1. **Analogue vs original:** Analogue gravity systems are laboratory analogues, not the original physical systems they model. Philosophical-of-science debate active (Thébault 2016 arXiv:1610.05028 "What Can We Learn From Analogue Experiments?"; Crowther et al. 2021 *European Journal for Philosophy of Science*). Relevance of analogue results to actual GR phenomena is methodologically contested. **UMF caveat:** importing analogue-gravity apparatus to UMF requires careful distinction between mathematical machinery (transferable) and physical-system claims (not directly transferable).
2. **Steinhauer 2016 BEC Hawking controversy:** Multiple challenges to interpretation (Leonhardt 2018 critique; various defences). Programme legitimacy not affected; specific observation status contested. **UMF caveat:** cite Steinhauer 2016 with honest acknowledgement of contested-observation status.
3. **Laboratory vs cosmological scale:** Most analogue systems are laboratory-scale (micrometres-metres); UMF operates at cosmological scale (Hubble radius). Scale-up is novel application requiring justification.
4. **Quantum-gravity question focus:** Analogue programme typically engages Hawking radiation + Unruh effect + cosmological particle creation in early universe. UMF G-shock matter creation is different (though related) question. Mathematical-framework overlap is substantive but not isomorphic at question level.
5. **No DM-candidate framework in core programme:** Analogue gravity does not provide non-standard DM mechanism. UMF gravitoelectric DM finds methodological-precedent only (legitimacy of laboratory-engaged programmatic gravity-foundations work), not mechanism precedent.
